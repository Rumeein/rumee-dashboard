Rumee — Stock & Cost Model

Oldest in, oldest out — how cost follows stock

LIVE — FIFO lot costing
2026-07-24

Every material used to have one blended average cost — buy the same thing at three different prices over time, and the Stock tab showed one smoothed-out number. Now each purchase or conversion batch keeps its own lot — its own date, its own cost — and whatever leaves stock (a sale, a conversion, a QC transfer, an adjustment) always draws from the oldest open lot first. Nothing moves to the next lot until the current one is fully used up.

oldest open lot -- what gets consumed next, and what "Next Cost/Unit" on the Stock tab shows a later, newer lot -- waits its turn one of the three stock buckets, each with its own lot queue
01

Every purchase or produced batch is its own lot

Buy 40 units of something on the 10th at ₹120, then 100 more on the 15th at ₹135 -- that's two separate lots, not one number that averages to ₹129. A conversion batch's output works the same way: it becomes a brand new lot, costed from what its own inputs actually cost to consume.

02

Whatever leaves stock draws from the oldest lot first

A marketplace sale, a conversion using this material as an input, a QC transfer between buckets, or a manual Stock Adjustment (out) -- all of them pull from the material's oldest open lot in that bucket. Once that lot hits zero, the next-oldest one takes over automatically. Nothing skips ahead.

03

Three buckets, three independent lot queues

Pending QC → Repair → Usable are the same three stock stages the Purchases/QC flow already uses (item #70, #83). Each one keeps its own FIFO queue. Moving qty from one bucket to another (a QC pass, a repair resolve) is really two movements: stock leaves the source bucket's oldest lot at that lot's real cost, and a new lot is created in the destination bucket at that exact same cost -- so cost basis travels with the physical units, it's never re-estimated along the way.

04

"Next Cost/Unit" on the Stock tab

The Stock tab's cost column shows the real cost of the oldest open lot -- the actual price the next unit sold or used will be costed at. If a material has lots at different costs, this number can change lot-to-lot rather than sitting still like an average did -- that's expected, not a bug: it's showing the true current cost, not a smoothed one.

✓

Existing history, reconstructed

The Stock Movements ledger already recorded every in/out movement ever made. A one-time, owner-only "Build FIFO Lot History" action on the Stock tab replays that ledger to build real lots retroactively -- any gap from stock that predates the ledger itself is folded into one clearly-labeled reconciling lot per material, never silently absorbed into the real history.

Shipped, live

Built as one connected change, since every stock-out in this app already funnels through a single function (postStockMovement):

  • New rumee_stock_lots collection -- one doc per lot, per material, per bucket.
  • Purchases, Conversions, QC transfers, and Stock Adjustments all create/consume real lots instead of blending one average.
  • QC transfers and Conversion output now cost themselves from the REAL FIFO cost of what was actually consumed, not a pre-read snapshot.
  • "Build FIFO Lot History" reconstructs real history from the existing Stock Movements ledger -- safe to re-run for newly-added materials.
Worth knowing

Any reconciling ("pre-ledger gap") lot the reconstruction creates is an estimate, not real history -- it only exists for stock that predates the ledger itself. The reconstruction step reports exactly which materials got one, so they can be sanity-checked rather than trusted blindly.
